# Specpunk Public Surface Concepts

## Question

If Specpunk should not use a conventional startup landing page, what kind of public surface should it have instead?

## Executive Summary

The research pack leaves **little freedom in message** and **a lot of freedom in form**.

The message is already constrained:

- Specpunk is a `repo-native intent-control and verification layer`
- for `AI-assisted brownfield development`
- sold as `review/risk/control infrastructure`
- with `portable core` and `Claude-first` enhancements
- under a `local-first` governance posture

What remains open is **how** that truth is surfaced publicly.

Best current synthesis:

1. `Boundary Demonstration` as the front door
2. `Public Lab Notebook` as the deep layer
3. `Control Room` as the product-truth layer

This is not one classic landing page. It is a three-layer public surface:

- first contact: immediate tension and recognition
- second layer: honest thinking, decisions, and unknowns
- third layer: the actual product object and its mechanics

The most promising non-fake interactions are:

- `Describe your last scary AI PR`
- `Open the artifact pack`
- `Request a conversation, not a demo`

## What Is Verified From The Current Research Pack

These constraints are already strongly supported by the local corpus:

1. Specpunk should be framed as a `repo-native intent-control and verification layer`, not as a new IDE, a new coding agent, or a spec-as-source compiler.
2. The strongest wedge is `review/risk/control infrastructure`, not "more code generation."
3. The product should not pretend to have uniform cross-tool parity; current posture is `portable core` with richer `Claude-first` enhancements.
4. Governance posture is already clear: `local-first`, `derived artifacts over raw transcripts`, `human-owned classification`.
5. The commercial audience is not "everyone"; the most plausible wedge is AI-heavy teams in shared brownfield codebases, with platform/DevEx/VP Eng as the likely buyer path.

## User-Specific Signals

These are not corpus facts. They are the user's direct inputs in this conversation:

- the page should make the right person `dig deeper`
- authorship can be muted or absent
- full rawness is acceptable
- standard startup CTA is suspicious
- fake certainty, social proof, meaningless numbers, and investor language feel false
- the ideal effect is closer to "you instantly get it" than to "you were persuaded"

This matters because it rules out large parts of the default startup landing grammar even if they are commercially common.

## Creative Synthesis: Three Surface Archetypes

## 1. Boundary Demonstration

### Core idea

The page is not a pitch. It is a single strong before/after experience:

- `without Specpunk`
- `with Specpunk`

Example scenario:

- agent touched `11` files instead of `3`
- reviewer sees a large diff with low confidence
- with Specpunk, the same change shows `scope`, `intent`, `evidence`, and a cleaner review decision

### What the visitor sees first

Not a hero paragraph. A conflict:

- uncontrolled AI change
- then controlled AI change

### Why it fits

- closest to the "I got ChatGPT immediately" effect the user described
- aligned with the research pack's strongest message: containment, reviewability, trust
- demonstrates product essence without startup-style persuasion

### Risk

- very sensitive to scenario quality
- if the demo scenario is weak, the whole public surface collapses

## 2. Public Lab Notebook

### Core idea

The public surface is a living record of:

- what the product is becoming
- what was learned
- what remains unproven
- what changed in the thesis

It shows an intelligence at work rather than pretending the product is already settled.

### What the visitor sees first

Not `Why Specpunk`.

Instead:

- current thesis delta
- latest benchmark learning
- current unknowns
- next build step

### Why it fits

- directly compatible with the user's rejection of fake certainty
- makes "dig deeper" the natural action
- turns the research pack into part of the public surface instead of hidden founder prep

### Risk

- may attract thinkers and peers more than buyers
- can drift into archive/wiki mode instead of product surface

## 3. Control Room

### Core idea

The public surface looks like the product's operating model:

- scope violations
- intent pack
- behavior evidence
- review artifact

Almost no hero copy. Mostly system state.

### What the visitor sees first

An interface or facsimile of one:

- panels
- states
- warnings
- evidence cards

### Why it fits

- the current research pack already frames Specpunk as infrastructure, not magic
- it is the most faithful rendering of the actual product shape

### Risk

- can feel too cold, internal, or abstract
- some visitors may not infer the problem without a narrative layer

## Recommended Composition

The strongest current direction is not to choose only one archetype.

Use them as layers:

### Layer 1: Boundary Demonstration

Purpose:

- instant recognition
- immediate problem/relief contrast

### Layer 2: Public Lab Notebook

Purpose:

- honesty
- depth
- founder thinking without startup theater

### Layer 3: Control Room

Purpose:

- show the product as a real object
- expose how it actually works

This composition preserves immediate grasp, honesty, and product-truth at the same time.

## Non-Fake Interaction Patterns

These are the most promising alternatives to default CTA logic:

## 1. Describe your last scary AI PR

Why good:

- collects real pain instead of vanity waitlist names
- signals seriousness
- aligns with review-risk positioning

Tradeoff:

- manual and low-scale

## 2. Open the artifact pack

Why good:

- lets the page hand over a real object, not a promise
- aligns with the `repo-native artifact` core

Tradeoff:

- some people will consume without responding

## 3. Request a conversation, not a demo

Why good:

- removes fake enterprise funnel tone
- matches founder-led discovery

Tradeoff:

- less predictable as a conversion mechanism

## 4. Disagree in public

Why good:

- invites tension and thought, not passive agreement
- fits the user's desire for something real rather than polished certainty

Tradeoff:

- noisy submissions and trolling risk

## 5. Read the thesis pack

Why good:

- filters for people willing to go deep
- makes the public surface intellectually honest

Tradeoff:

- very low conventional conversion

## What The Surface Must Not Pretend

Do not present Specpunk as:

- an AI coding tool
- a magic reasoning layer with equal support everywhere
- a cloud transcript mining service by default
- a universal product for all developers
- a speed/throughput tool first

Do not lean on:

- social proof
- investor language
- meaningless numbers
- polished certainty that the current research does not justify

## Open Questions

1. Which concrete scenario should power the `Boundary Demonstration`?
2. Should the very first screen have any text at all, or only a conflict/resolution artifact?
3. Does the public surface need a visible author, or should the author stay mostly absent?
4. Which of the non-fake interactions produces signal without feeling like a funnel?

## Recommended Next Step

Do not start with a conventional marketing landing.

Start with a `public surface brief` that defines:

1. the single scenario for the `Boundary Demonstration`
2. the structure of the `Public Lab Notebook`
3. the minimum `Control Room` representation
4. one primary interaction pattern to test

After that, implementation can begin directly from the brief instead of from generic landing-page templates.
